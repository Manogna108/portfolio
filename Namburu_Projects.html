Solar Sisters

For this project the given task was that Tesla Motors was investigating the use of solar and hydrogen cell to power their cars. The company wanted to gain some insight on the amount of power and speed capabilities of these sources to support their designs. In a group of four, my team and were instructed (1.3.2 Instructions) to design, create, and test a prototype of a car that uses a solar module and/or hydrogen fuel cell as its power source. We had to use different combinations of energy sources such as 1 hydrogen fuel cell, 2 hydrogen fuel cells in a parallel or series circuit, 1 solar cell, and 2 solar cells in a parallel or series circuit to see which configuration had the best results in time and efficiency. As a team we built a two level vehicle that can potentially hold two solar cells along with two hydrogen cells. As we tested out our different configurations we realized that our prototype works best with two solar cells compared to any other configurations. In our Documentation we noted all the changes and results of our different power configurations such as the efficiency of each type energy source. In this project my role was to build the compartments on the vehicle so that the solar and hydrogen cells will not just be placed on the vehicle but be mechanically built in. I was also incharge of working on the calculations of each power source and coming to a conclusion of which source was the best. As I worked on this project I learned more about the difficulties using renewable sources for a long period of time. Both solar cells and hydrogen cells have both pros and cons to them. Solar cells might generally be more efficient than hydrogen cells but the problem with them are that they do not store energy from the UV light and as a result they fail to work without a constant power source. Hydrogen cells do store energy in them but have to constantly be charged and work at a very slow pace. Some challenges with this project was with the hydrogen cells which sometimes worked but other times took quite a long time to even move. My group and I tried to make the efficient of the hydrogen cell improve but were unable to do so within our deadline. Overall, with this project I learned to use two different energy sources to power a vehicle.

Type of project:

West Point Bridge Design

In this project, my partner and I had to create a stable bridge for the American Bridge company while still keeping the design within the budget and following the constraints. Each of us had to create the two designs using materials such as carbon steel, high-strength low-alloy steel, or quenched along with a cross section of the solid or hollow bar. For my first design, I used the Pratt truss design made of carbon steel with a cross-section of a solid bar. The size of each truss varies between 130mm to 150mm. The cost of this truss was $299.597.72 which was under the budget of $300,000. However, this bridge was not very stable since every time a truck went over it bends inwards a little too much. The second design was a Howe Truss design made of high strength low alloy with the cross section of Hollow tube. The size of each truss varies between 140mm to 220mm. The cost was a little more expensive than my first one since it was around $299,870.52. This bridge was much more stable than the first one since it was much secured and bent very little with the weight of the truck over it. To finalize which bridge to use, we created a table in our Documentation that compares all four designs in different aspects such as cost, secureness, movement, and compression/tension. Once we finished our designing and finalizing I worked on the decision matrix and research summary. The decision matrix is a paragraph about why we chose that bridge which could be backed up by the table. Through the research summary, it was found that most bridge designs are Pratt and Howe in which the Pratt is the best choice for it covered more distance. Through this project, I learned the different types of bridge designs there are and how materials and sizes determine the effectiveness of a bridge.

Android Studios Skeleton App

In this project, my partners and I were assigned to create a skeleton of an app in Android Studios. This project was intended to help us get familiar with the environment present in Android Studios. As we were given the creative freedom to choose any topic for our app, my partners and I came up with an app that would help people of all ages to track their health habits. This app is tailored to give patients a clear and easy way to know how much of their daily intake of cholesterol, fats, carbs, sodium, and sugars they have consumed. And based on that, we give them some possible activities they can follow to help control their health issues. Our brainstorming and designing were all done in our Documentation where we decided that we would have a login screen, setting screen, one activity screen for the user to input their intake, and another activity screen for the app to give them recommendations based on their dietary habits. However, we did not have enough time to implement our two activity screens as we wanted. Instead we had one nutrition advice screen, that suggested some healthy meal guidelines and another exercise screen, that gave some basic exercise suggestions. In this project I worked on creating the login screen and the last activity screen. For the login screen, I created a basic interface where the user can include their username and password and then click a button called login. Since this is a skeleton app, the username and password do not actually get stored but ideally this is how a person would login onto our app. The next page is the setting screen. The user can select if they would like a dark mode for the app, if they would like any sound and if they want to save these preferences so the next time they login, they can skip the settings page. After they pick their settings, they can click next to go to the next screen. The next screen is the nutrition intake screen. In this screen the user can check off the boxes of guidelines they have followed. They then can click next to go to the final screen. In the final screen, the exercise screen, I provided some basic exercises that people of all ages could follow. Once again the user would have to check the boxes to indicate they have completed a certain task. Once the user clicks next, it would take them back to the settings screen and start all over. Through this project, I learned to use android studios to build a basic outline of a working app and to work with a different type of code and infrastructure.

Chatbot Application

In this project, my partners and I were assigned to create a demo of a chatbot that does anything of our choice. It has to be able to carry a conversation with the user with at least 35 input/response pairs. We decided to create a project that will function as a virtual companion that listens to the user’s situation and responds accordingly. In our Documentation we listed all the types of inputs we want to include as well as some possible outputs. Our chatbot, EMO or also known as emotional support chatbot, responds to the user after it evaluates their statement into one of the six categories we assigned it. The six categories are all different types of emotions the user may feel such as fear, love, anger, sadness, happiness and even any physical pain. If one of these categories gets called on such as love, a generic statement would be sent to the user about love. Within each category, 5-6 keywords would trigger another generic response that gets added unto the category’s generic statement. For example, if the keyword laugh is included in the user’s input, the chatbot would first produce a generic statement about happiness since laugh is a keyword included for happiness and another statement specifically for laugh. A clear demo of how a basic conversation would could be seen in our demo. This project was intended to help us get better at using Android Studios along with getting introduced to some simple functions of AI applications. Through this project, I learned how to get a chatbot to simulate a human conversation by including certain keywords and such. I also got the chance to work with multiple interfaces to do one overall function.
