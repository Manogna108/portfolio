Solar Sisters

For this project the given task was that Tesla Motors was investigating the use of solar and hydrogen cell to power their cars. The company wanted to gain some insight on the amount of power and speed capabilities of these sources to support their designs. In a group of four, my team and were instructed (1.3.2 Instructions) to design, create, and test a prototype of a car that uses a solar module and/or hydrogen fuel cell as its power source. We had to use different combinations of energy sources such as 1 hydrogen fuel cell, 2 hydrogen fuel cells in a parallel or series circuit, 1 solar cell, and 2 solar cells in a parallel or series circuit to see which configuration had the best results in time and efficiency. As a team we built a two level vehicle that can potentially hold two solar cells along with two hydrogen cells. As we tested out our different configurations we realized that our prototype works best with two solar cells compared to any other configurations. In our Documentation we noted all the changes and results of our different power configurations such as the efficiency of each type energy source. In this project my role was to build the compartments on the vehicle so that the solar and hydrogen cells will not just be placed on the vehicle but be mechanically built in. I was also incharge of working on the calculations of each power source and coming to a conclusion of which source was the best. As I worked on this project I learned more about the difficulties using renewable sources for a long period of time. Both solar cells and hydrogen cells have both pros and cons to them. Solar cells might generally be more efficient than hydrogen cells but the problem with them are that they do not store energy from the UV light and as a result they fail to work without a constant power source. Hydrogen cells do store energy in them but have to constantly be charged and work at a very slow pace. Some challenges with this project was with the hydrogen cells which sometimes worked but other times took quite a long time to even move. My group and I tried to make the efficient of the hydrogen cell improve but were unable to do so within our deadline. Overall, with this project I learned to use two different energy sources to power a vehicle.

Chatbot Application

In this project, my partners and I were assigned to create a demo of a chatbot that does anything of our choice. It has to be able to carry a conversation with the user with at least 35 input/response pairs. We decided to create a project that will function as a virtual companion that listens to the userâ€™s situation and responds accordingly. In our Documentation we listed all the types of inputs we want to include as well as some possible outputs. Our chatbot, EMO or also known as emotional support chatbot, responds to the user after it evaluates their statement into one of the six categories we assigned it. The six categories are all different types of emotions the user may feel such as fear, love, anger, sadness, happiness and even any physical pain. If one of these categories gets called on such as love, a generic statement would be sent to the user about love. Within each category, 5-6 keywords would trigger another generic response that gets added unto the categoryâ€™s generic statement. For example, if the keyword laugh is included in the userâ€™s input, the chatbot would first produce a generic statement about happiness since laugh is a keyword included for happiness and another statement specifically for laugh. A clear demo of how a basic conversation would could be seen in our demo. This project was intended to help us get better at using Android Studios along with getting introduced to some simple functions of AI applications. Through this project, I learned how to get a chatbot to simulate a human conversation by including certain keywords and such.
