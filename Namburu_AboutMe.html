Manogna Namburu

My name is Manogna Namburu, and I am a senior at Dublin High School. I am part of the engineering academy, specifically the computer science pathway. My journey started in my Freshman year when my parents urged me to take Computer Science Principles as an elective. At first, I was hesitant since I have never tried coding and do not know the first thing about computer science. However, since my parents are computer science engineers, I always had that curiosity regarding what they do. With that in mind, I took the course. The first thing I learned in that class was that computer science is taking real-world problems and instructing the computer on how to solve them. The simplicity and creativity of the solution all depend on the programmer, and the simpler the code is, the better. I liked the idea of trying to improve existing solutions and felt motivated to try making things better and effective. My passion for Computer Science only grew after taking Computer Science Application in my Junior year.
I am generally seen as a person who works hard to reach her goals and does not give up on the way because the workload is too much. Even at school projects, I am self-motivated to get the work done on time. I possess characteristics such as being alert, organized, and persistent; once I get engaged in some activity, I do not back out. One of my favorite hobbies is swimming. I started swimming when I was six years old. At first, I had an extreme fear of letting go of the wall of the pool in case I drown. No matter how many times my swim instructor tried to convince me, I refused to trust her and let go. In the end, I could only practice my kicks while watching the other students swim freely. I wanted to be in the middle of the pool, learning more than kicking and hand movements. With that goal in mind, I went to the neighborhood pool to try practicing on my own with no one around. I first started waddling, holding onto the wall with one hand. Then after some time, I let go and swam slowly, still close to the wall.
